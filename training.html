Training & Education for Electrical Engineers

Typical education paths include a Bachelor's in Electrical Engineering (B.S. or B.E.). Coursework often covers circuits, signals and systems, electronics, electromagnetics, power systems, control systems, and embedded systems.

  • Practical experience: Lab courses, PCB projects, microcontroller programming, and senior design projects.
  • Advanced study: Master's degrees and specialized certificates for robotics, power electronics, RF, or embedded systems can boost career options.
  • Online resources: Coursera, edX, and vendor training (NI, Keysight) for tools and domain-specific skills.
